Activity 2.2.3.

Some LaTeX notation is rather straight-forward, with literal notation such as + and -, and intuitive notation such as ^ for superscripts and _ for subscripts: a_0+a_1^2=-a_2^3 produces \(a_0+a_1^2=-a_2^3\text{.}\)

But multiplication cannot be represented by, say, x, since that represents the variable \(x\text{.}\) (Instead we write 2 \times 3 to produce \(2 \times 3\text{.}\))

Note that z_123 produces \(z_123\text{,}\) so grouping in LaTeX may be accomplished with curly braces {}: z_{123} produces \(z_{123}\text{.}\) Actual curly braces may be displayed as follows: \{1,2,3\} produces \(\{1,2,3\}\text{.}\)

Even professionals usually know just a small subset of all the LaTeX commands available. Instead, references like KaTeX's Support Table and tools like DeTeXify allow for easy lookup of LaTeX commands as needed.

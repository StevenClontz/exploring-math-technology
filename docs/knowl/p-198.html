While SageMath does its best to hide this limitation from users, having finitely-many possible numbers is not ideal for data intended for storage on a physical computer. To account for this, some lower-level languages offer data types like the short that account for a useful (but finite) range of integers. In particular, shorts are integers that may be stored as a short finite list of the binary digits \(0\) and \(1\text{.}\) (Each binary digit is also known as a bit.)

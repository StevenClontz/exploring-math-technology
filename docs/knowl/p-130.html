Many mathematicians believe (but no one has been able to prove) that the circular constant \(\pi=3.14159\cdots\) is a “normal” number, which means that every finite sequence of digits will appear eventually in its decimal expression. For example, the SageMath code pi.n(digits=10) reveals that \(65\) appears as the 7th and 8th decimal digits of \(\pi\text{.}\)
